## Executive Summary

The Marketing department is structured as a lean, 7-person team across three units—Architect Growth, Content & Brand, and Growth Operations—optimized for our architect-first, invite-only beta strategy. Our primary focus for the first 90 days is onboarding 50 architects, achieving 70% activation rate, and establishing a viral loop where each architect brings at least 1.5 additional users. All marketing efforts lead with the annotation workspace as the core product differentiator, positioning HouseMind as "the shared workspace for building decisions" rather than another marketplace.

---

## Key Decisions Made

### 1. Team Structure: Hybrid User-Segment + Shared Services

**Decision:** Three teams with clear ownership boundaries rather than functional silos or full pod model.

| Team | Headcount | Primary Focus |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Architect Growth | 2 | Acquisition, activation, viral loop |
| Content & Brand | 3 | Messaging, content production, brand consistency |
| Growth Operations | 2 | Analytics, automation, marketing infrastructure |

**Rationale:** Architect-first strategy demands dedicated ownership of the architect journey. Shared services (design, analytics) avoid resource duplication while maintaining consistency.

---

### 2. Architect-First Acquisition Strategy

**Decision:** All beta acquisition flows through architect relationships—no homeowner or contractor direct acquisition in Phase 1.

**Approach:**
- Co-owner personal network as primary invite source
- Community & partnership development as secondary source
- Referral program to amplify architect-to-architect growth
- Viral loop (architect → homeowner/contractor) as organic expansion mechanism

**Rationale:** Architects are decision-makers who pull homeowners and contractors into projects. Acquiring architects first creates natural downstream adoption.

---

### 3. Channel Prioritization

**Decision:** Prioritize high-touch, relationship-driven channels over paid acquisition during beta.

| Priority | Channel | Investment Level |
|----------|---------|------------------|
| 1 | Direct invites (co-owner + Growth Lead) | HIGH |
| 2 | Architect communities (LINE groups, Facebook) | HIGH |
| 3 | Professional associations (ASA) | MEDIUM |
| 4 | Events/workshops | MEDIUM |
| 5 | Paid acquisition | LOW (Phase 2) |

**Rationale:** Invite-only beta limits scalable channels. High-touch acquisition builds relationships for case studies, referrals, and product feedback.

---

### 4. Messaging Positioning

**Decision:** Position HouseMind as a collaboration workspace, explicitly differentiated from marketplaces and inspiration platforms.

| Positioning Element | Approach |
|---------------------|----------|
| Primary frame | "The shared workspace for building decisions" |
| Key differentiator | Annotation workspace where decisions happen |
| Against Pinterest/Houzz | "Real decisions, not endless browsing" |
| Against LINE/WhatsApp | "Structured visual workspace, not chat chaos" |
| Value proposition (architects) | "Show, don't tell. Get client buy-in before the first brick is laid." |

**Rationale:** Marketplace positioning attracts tire-kickers. Workspace positioning attracts professionals who need to get work done.
