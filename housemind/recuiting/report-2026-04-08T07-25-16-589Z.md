# Operations Department Report

## Executive Summary

Operations will run as a lean 5-person department structured into two teams: User Success & QA (handling invites, onboarding, feedback, and quality assurance) and Catalog & Supply (handling product curation and supplier recruitment). All processes are designed for manual execution at beta scale (50-100 users) with documented triggers for when to hire or automate. The department can be operational within 4 weeks pending Tech delivery of admin panel functionality and team hiring.

---

## Key Decisions Made

### 1. Team Structure

| Decision | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| **2 teams, not 3+** | Minimizes coordination overhead at beta scale. Clear ownership boundaries. |
| **5 roles total** | 45-65 hrs/week workload distributed across 4 specialists + 1 lead prevents burnout and allows coverage. |
| **User Success + QA combined** | Natural overlap—both are user-facing, both feed insights to product. QA person interacts with users during bug verification. |
| **Catalog + Supply combined** | Catalog curation drives supplier recruitment. Curator understands product gaps and can inform outreach priorities. |
| **No dedicated invite manager** | Volume too low to justify. User Success Lead absorbs this with tooling support. |

### 2. Role Design

| Role | Team | Weekly Hours | Key Ownership |
|------|------|--------------|---------------|
| Head of Operations | — | 22-30 | Strategy, escalations, cross-department coordination |
| User Success Lead | User Success & QA | 20-27 | Invites, onboarding monitoring, feedback calls |
| QA & Feedback Coordinator | User Success & QA | 18-24 | Device testing, bug documentation, localization QA |
| Catalog Curator | Catalog & Supply | 22-30 | Product sourcing, data entry, quality standards |
| Supplier Relations Coordinator | Catalog & Supply | 14-21 | Demand analysis, supplier outreach, onboarding |

### 3. Process Decisions

| Process | Decision | Rationale |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| **Invite flow** | Manual token creation via admin panel, personalized outreach via WhatsApp/LINE | High-touch appropriate for invite-only beta |
| **Onboarding monitoring** | Daily dashboard check with staged interventions | Catch drop-offs early without over-automating |
| **Feedback calls** | 15-min structured calls at day 7, logged in Notion/Airtable | Balances depth with scalability |
| **Bug reporting** | Standardized template, severity classification by QA, Tech triages | Clear handoff, reduces back-and-forth |
| **Supplier outreach** | Demand-driven (based on "request availability" clicks) | Efficient use of limited outreach capacity |
| **Catalog labeling** | "Reference" default, "Available" only after supplier confirmation | Prevents false availability promises |

### 4. Tooling Decisions

| Tool | Purpose | Decision |
|------|---------|----------|
| Notion or Airtable | Feedback tracking, supplier pipeline, product backlog | Use existing company preference; either works |
| Linear | Bug tracking | Aligns with Tech; single source of truth |
| Calendly/Cal.com | Feedback call scheduling | Free tier sufficient for beta |
| WhatsApp Business + LINE | User and supplier communication | Standard channels in Thai market |
| Physical devices | QA testing | Samsung A14/A24, iPhone SE, iPad base model (~$600 one-time) |

### 5. Scaling Triggers

| Trigger | Threshold | Action |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| Active users | >50 | Hire User Success Associate |
| Active users | >100 | Build self-serve waitlist, automate onboarding nudges |
| Products in catalog | >200 | Hire junior curator or automate data entry |
| Active suppliers | >10 | Hire dedicated Supplier Account Manager |
| Releases per week | >2 | Hire QA Associate or invest in automated testing |

## Risks and Open Questions for PM

### Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation | Owner |
|------|------------|--------|------------|-------|
| **Admin panel not ready for Week 2** | Medium | High | Prioritize with Tech; create manual workaround if needed | Head of Operations + Tech Lead |
| **Hiring takes longer than 2 weeks** | Medium | High | Start recruiting immediately; have backup plan to operate with 3 roles initially | Head of Operations |
| **Low invite acceptance rate** | Medium | Medium | Test messaging variations; leverage co-owner personal relationships | User Success Lead |
| **Suppliers unresponsive to cold outreach** | High | Medium | Pursue warm intros via architects; offer clear beta value prop | Supplier Relations Coordinator |
| **QA misses critical bug before release** | Low | High | Standardized test checklist; require sign-off before production deploy | QA Coordinator |
| **Single point of failure (any role)** | Medium | High | Cross-train basics; document all processes; I provide backup coverage | Head of Operations |
| **Unclear boundary between Ops and Tech on bug ownership** | Medium | Medium | Document interface; establish weekly sync; escalation path defined | Head of Operations + Tech Lead |

### Open Questions for PM

| Question | Context | Impact if Unresolved |
|----------|---------|----------------------|
| **What is the headcount budget?** | I've designed for 5 roles (4 + me). Need confirmation this is approved. | Cannot begin hiring |
| **Are roles human, AI agents, or hybrid?** | Document assumes human. If AI agents are expected, scope changes significantly. | Entire structure may need redesign |
| **What is the timeline for first beta users?** | Deliverables assume users arrive Week 3-4. If sooner, we need to accelerate. | Ops not ready for users |
| **Who owns the relationship with co-owner for invite decisions?** | Ops executes invites, but co-owner decides who to invite. Need clear handoff. | Confusion on invite pipeline |
| **Is there budget for tools (~$50-100/month) and devices (~$600 one-time)?** | Required for QA and workflow management. | QA and tracking compromised |
| **What is Tech's release cadence?** | QA planning depends on knowing how often releases ship. | QA capacity planning unclear |
| **Who owns product decisions influenced by user feedback?** | Ops collects feedback; who prioritizes what gets built? | Feedback may not lead to action |
| **What happens when a supplier wants commercial terms?** | Current plan is free during beta. When do we negotiate? Who owns pricing? | Supplier conversations stall |
