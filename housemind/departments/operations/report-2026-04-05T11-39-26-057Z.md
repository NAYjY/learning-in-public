# Operations Department Report: Product Catalog Curation Plan

## **Executive Summary**
The HouseMind Operations team has developed a detailed plan to manually curate 100–150 reference products across Tiles/Flooring, Kitchen Fixtures/Hardware, Lighting, and Exterior Cladding categories. The plan prioritizes functionality for architects during the beta phase by focusing on diverse yet manageable product selections, high-quality data formats, and structured onboarding for beta users. Key systems are being put in place to ensure seamless sourcing, quality assurance, and user feedback, while fostering cross-departmental collaboration to facilitate a successful launch and continuous improvement.

---

## **Key Decisions Made**
1. Curate **100–150 reference products** with a targeted balance of categories:  
   - **Tiles/Flooring**: 30-40 products  
   - **Kitchen Fixtures/Hardware**: 25-30 products  
   - **Lighting**: 25-30 products  
   - **Exterior Cladding**: 20-25 products  

2. Implement a standardized **product data format** with clear columns for specifications, metadata (e.g., Reference/Available labels), and consistent high-quality visual assets.

3. Source products from a mix of **20 reputable suppliers/brands**, leveraging publicly available catalogs and establishing lightweight collaboration agreements for product data usage.

4. Develop a **four-step onboarding checklist** for architects in beta, supported by:
   - Prebuilt example project boards.  
   - Video walkthroughs/tutorials.  
   - 15-minute feedback calls.  

5. Track beta usage using **key metrics** (e.g., catalog views, boards created/shared, product selection) via an analytics dashboard to identify areas for improvement.

## **Risks and Open Questions for PM**
1. **Risk 1: Backend Constraints**  
   - **Challenge**: Ensuring the curated product data format (spreadsheet) is compatible with the backend system for uploading into the platform.  
   - **Mitigation**: Collaborate with the Product Team early to confirm format requirements and conduct data compatibility tests during the curation process.  

2. **Risk 2: Supplier Participation in Beta**  
   - **Challenge**: Ensuring we get timely approval from suppliers to include their data in the beta catalog. Delays may slow down completion of the catalog.  
   - **Mitigation**: Utilize lightweight Beta Collaboration Agreements and prioritize sourcing products from suppliers with public catalogs, if needed.

3. **Risk 3: Beta Architect Drop-Off**  
   - **Challenge**: If the product catalog or onboarding workflow fails to meet architect needs, there may be drop-offs in engagement during the beta testing phase.  
   - **Mitigation**: Conduct onboarding support calls, highlight curated product value, and run structured feedback loops in the first week.

4. **Open Questions:**
   - **Product Integration:** Are there any specific constraints on how the catalog data needs to be formatted and uploaded into the platform backend?  
   - **Search Functionality:** Will architects have advanced search filters (e.g., price range, color) during beta, or do we need to adjust data format to account for future feature rollout?  
   - **Onboarding Features:** Can we create a first-use guided walkthrough for architects within the platform, or should we lean entirely on external videos and onboarding calls?
