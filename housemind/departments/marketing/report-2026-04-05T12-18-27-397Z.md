## Executive Summary

The MVP aligns well with HouseMind’s architect-first strategy but lacks some polish and content to fully support the platform’s value proposition as a collaboration-first tool. The key focus for marketing is to enhance onboarding, clarify the value proposition, improve the product catalog, and strengthen features that reinforce the viral loop. ## Key Decisions Made
1. The landing page messaging will be updated to reflect the value proposition: “The Shared Workspace for Building Decisions.”
2. Architect leadership will be emphasized through UI updates on project boards such as “Lead Architect” labels and intuitive CTAs.
3. Onboarding flow will be augmented with a welcome checklist, in-app walkthrough, and email drip campaigns highlighting the platform’s value and first steps for architects.
4. Product catalog improvements will focus on better product images, clearer descriptions, architecture-specific filters, and the addition of short explanation tooltips.
5. Viral growth will be supported by a streamlined project board sharing flow, collaborator notifications, and feedback prompts at critical SaaS friction points.

## Risks and Open Questions for Product Management
1. **Catalog Quality:** Are the seed products in the catalog launch-ready? High-quality images and detailed descriptions are vital for architect engagement. If not, will Product/Ops be able to curate and upload appropriate content before beta invites begin?  
2. **In-App Product Walkthrough Implementation:** Does Engineering have bandwidth to prioritize embedding an onboarding walkthrough for first-time users, potentially with a service like Appcues or Tourkit?  
3. **Notifications and Feedback Implementation:** The MVP lacks user engagement features, such as notifications for collaborators joining boards or prompts for feedback. Can we include these features in Wave 2, and what dependencies would this entail?  
4. **Feedback Scalability:** How quickly can the Product team act on feedback from beta users? Rapid iteration will be critical for retaining early adopters.
