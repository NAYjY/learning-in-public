### Community Manager Output: Early Testing and Feedback Strategy for Annotation Workspace

---

#### **1. Identify Beta Testers and Segment User Pools**
- **Objective:** Ensure a diverse beta tester pool for thorough testing of the annotation workspace.
- **Execution Plan:**
  1. Work with the Product and Growth teams to identify top-performing architects, contractors, and homeowners from the current beta program.
  2. Categorize testers into segments:
     - **Architects (Power Users):** Experienced, engaged users who are already active on the platform. Prioritize architects who use the comment feature often.
     - **Contractors (Mobile-First Users):** Select contractors who engage with the project boards and have provided feedback in the past.
     - **Homeowners (Secondary Users):** Invite homeowners who have shown interest in project feedback and collaboration.
  3. Aim for 75-100 total testers, with 50% architects, 30% contractors, and 20% homeowners.
  4. Utilize existing communication channels (e.g., LINE, email, and social media) to recruit and onboard these testers.

---

#### **2. Facilitate A/B Testing and Feedback Loops**
- **Objective:** Gather actionable, structured feedback on the annotation workspace through organized testing methods.
- **Execution Plan:**
  1. **A/B Testing:** 
     - Test different emoji annotation styles (e.g., professional vs. whimsical) and pin designs for clarity and appeal.
     - Present two UI versions of the circular emoji menu and product linking flow to determine user preferences.
  2. **Feedback Collection:**
     - Enable a quick, targeted popup survey in the workspace:
       - Example: “How easy was it to add an annotation (1-5)?”
       - Example: “What feedback do you have about the emoji tool?”
     - Schedule biweekly 1:1 interviews with 5 power users to dive deeper into their experiences.
     - Monitor usage data for key actions (e.g., time spent per session, time to complete key milestones like first annotation added or product linked).
  3. **Record Frustration Triggers:**
     - Establish a shared repository for team members to log common user complaints about features, usability issues, or recurring bugs.
     - Address pressing issues within 24-48 hours of reporting and notify users of progress to maintain engagement.

---

#### **3. User Engagement & Announcements**
- **Objective:** Keep the community excited and engaged with product updates while humanizing the rollout process.
- **Execution Plan:**
  1. **Pre-Rollout Teasers:**
     - Leverage LINE Official Account and a private Facebook group to share short preview videos of the workspace features in action.
     - Use posts such as: *“Something new and exciting is coming to HouseMind! Get ready for a feature that will bring your projects to life. Can you guess what it is?”*  
        *[Accompany this with a blurred image or a video teaser showcasing emoji annotations.]* 
  2. **Launch Announcements:**
     - Send targeted release emails in both Thai and English to beta testers, highlighting the new features and embedded tutorial links.
     - Host a walkthrough webinar for architects and contractors: *“Master collaboration with the HouseMind Annotation Workspace”*.
     - Feature key functionality on the platform’s landing page with dedicated GIFs/videos explaining the visual annotation system.
  3. **Weekly Tips & Tricks:**
     - Share practical tips via LINE/Facebook/Social Media:
       - Week 1: *“Use 🔄 to highlight parts of your design that need more iteration.”*
       - Week 2: *“Link a product to your pinned annotations—click the pin for sourcing options!”*

---

#### **4. Rollout Support and Feedback Collection**
- **Objective:** Ensure users receive timely support and feel empowered to contribute feedback, with clear communication around issue resolution.
- **Execution Plan:**
  1. **Multi-Channel Support Setup:**
     - Utilize in-app chat support for real-time assistance. Add a chatbot for first-line responses, escalating to a live agent for unresolved issues.
     - Provide a support email for detailed problem descriptions and attachments.
     - Include a robust FAQ section in-app, using the Content Writer’s microcopy as a starting point.
  2. **Feedback Tool Integration:**
     - Embed a workspace-specific feedback tool:
       - Category options: “Bug Report,” “Feature Request,” “Ease of Use Feedback,” “Other.”
       - Optional feedback description: *“Want us to call you to discuss this issue? Click here to schedule.”*
  3. **Response & Escalation SLA:**
     - Initial issue acknowledgment: within 1 hour during the pilot testing phase.
     - Issue resolution: within 1-2 business days.
     - High-priority issues escalate directly to PM within 24 hours.

---

#### **5. Early Warning System**
- **Objective:** Monitor user frustration or churn indicators to identify risks early.
- **Execution Plan:**
  1. **Usage Metrics Monitoring:**
     - Track metrics in the annotation workspace:
       - Number of annotations per user per session.
       - Time spent engaging with annotations.
       - Drop-off points in the user flow (e.g., adding an image → pinning an annotation → product linking).
     - Send automated follow-up reminders for users who fail to engage with the feature within their first 3 sessions.
  2. **Sentiment Analysis:**
     - Periodically analyze user feedback for non-explicit frustration signals (e.g., negative sentiment words such as “difficult,” “confusing,” or “don’t get it”).
  3. **Community Check-Ins:**
     - Conduct brief weekly surveys in LINE groups asking: 
       - *“Have you tried our new annotation workspace yet? If so, what do you think?”*
       - Include direct links to leave feedback.
     - Engage with users who haven’t tried the feature, offer step-by-step guidance or ask for factors holding them back.

---

#### **6. Community Guidelines and Inspiration**
- **Objective:** Establish clear expectations and highlight exceptional user contributions to inspire others.
- **Execution Plan:**
  - **Community Guidelines:**
    - Collaborate with team members to draft rules focusing on:
      - Respectful communication and constructive feedback.
      - Usage best practices for the annotation tools.
      - No off-topic or unrelated products spam in annotations.
  - **Role Models:**
    - Spotlight beta users who actively use and contribute valuable feedback during the testing phase. Example posts: *“Meet our Annotation Star of the Week: [User Name] — Here’s how they’re using emoji annotations for success!”*  
  - **Featured Projects:**
    - Share weekly featured projects on Facebook and via newsletters to inspire other beta testers.

---

2. Scheduled pre-rollout campaign: teaser posts, exclusive previews, and signup prompts.
3. Draft community **guidelines and engagement strategies**.
4. Framework for multi-channel **support and escalation SLA policies**.
5. Feedback categories defined for the workspace tool (aligned with UX Research recommendations).

   - Week 1: Initial rollout messaging (announcements, tool previews, email blast).
   - Week 2: Early feedback survey. Emoji guide (best practices post).
   - Week 3: Beta user spotlight + follow-up Q&A.
   - Week 4: Inspirational "how-to" content + release of a user-generated success story.
