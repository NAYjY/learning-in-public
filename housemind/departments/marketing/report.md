# **HouseMind Marketing Department Report: Architect Beta Launch Plan**

## **Executive Summary**
We are preparing for the invite-only beta launch of HouseMind targeted exclusively to architects, with direct invitations coming from the co-owner. The campaign is focused on positioning HouseMind as the premier "shared workspace for building decisions" to solve architects' pain points in product selection and collaboration. This plan outlines the key decisions, deliverables, risks, and cross-departmental dependencies needed to execute a successful launch that drives engagement and positions beta users as evangelists for organic growth.

---

## **Key Decisions Made**
1. **Audience Profile**: Focus on mid-to-senior architects (1–20 employee firms) specializing in residential design, especially in innovative metro markets (e.g., NY, SF). Target socially active professionals for viral potential.
2. **Messaging Strategy**: Highlight HouseMind as a collaborative, time-saving tool that eliminates inefficiencies in product selection processes. Emphasize exclusivity and the opportunity to "shape the future of collaboration."
3. **Invitation Process**: Leverage a high-touch, personal approach with invites from the co-owner, a dedicated beta landing page, live onboarding sessions, and consistent nurture emails.
4. **Beta Highlight Metrics**: Focus on acceptance rate (20–30%), activity rate (≥60% creating project boards), and collaboration rate (~2 collaborators/board) to assess success.
5. **Evangelism Strategy**: Position beta architects as key contributors to the platform's future, reward their advocacy, and establish social capital with public recognition (case studies, badges, testimonials).

## **Risks and Open Questions for Product Management**
### **Risks:**
1. **Low Engagement**: New users may lose interest if onboarding is unclear or if the product doesn’t immediately address key pain points.  
   - **PM Input Needed**: Are there any usability bottlenecks or key areas of functionality that could impact adoption? How can we address these proactively?  

2. **Limited Product Features in Beta**: Without key collaborative features (e.g., messaging or homeowner/contractor integration), the product may feel incomplete.  
   - **PM Input Needed**: Will the current product version have sufficient features to deliver the promised value during the beta?

3. **Overreliance on co-owner's network**: A small number of invites may limit our ability to collect representative feedback or generate a viral loop.  
   - **PM Input Needed**: If engagement metrics fall below expectations, how quickly can additional invite avenues be explored or implemented?

### **Open Questions:**
1. What is the timeline for completing the product enhancements that architects might request during beta?  
2. Are there any specific technical limitations or identified bugs in the beta that should be addressed upfront?  
3. How will product usage data (e.g., board creation, collaboration rates, feature utilization) be captured? Do we need to define custom event tracking beforehand?
